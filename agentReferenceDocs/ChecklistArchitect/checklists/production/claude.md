# Production Checklists - Manufacturing Reality Cable Network

## Overview: Production as Design-Reality Bridge Cables
Production checklists create quality assurance cables between design specifications and manufacturing reality. These are the "bridge cables" that ensure designed products can actually be manufactured within quality, cost, and timeline constraints.

## Just-in-Time Context Access
Load production checklists when:
- **Design-for-Manufacturing (DFM)** validation needed (early design phases)
- **Production readiness assessment** (Gate 3 and Gate 4 transitions)
- **Manufacturing method selection** affecting design constraints
- **Production scaling** from prototype to volume manufacturing
- **Quality control** system design and validation

### Production Cables as Manufacturing Bridges
Production checklists create "manufacturing bridge cables":
- **Design-to-Production Cables**: Connect design specifications to manufacturing processes
- **Quality Control Cables**: Link design requirements to production verification
- **Cost-Performance Cables**: Balance design optimization with manufacturing economics
- **Scalability Cables**: Ensure designs work from prototype through volume production

### Primary Production Cable Categories

#### Design for Manufacturing (DFM) Cables
**Function**: Ensure designs can be manufactured with available processes
**Network Location**: Design Decisions → Manufacturing Feasibility
**When Critical**: Early design phases, complex geometries, tight tolerances

**Cable Verification Requirements**:
- Feature manufacturability within process capabilities
- Tolerance requirements achievable with available equipment
- Material compatibility with manufacturing processes
- Assembly sequence validated for production environment

#### Quality Control Integration Cables
**Function**: Connect design requirements to production verification
**Network Location**: Design Specifications → Quality Verification
**When Critical**: Quality-critical features, regulatory compliance, customer requirements

**Cable Verification Requirements**:
- Critical dimensions have inspection procedures
- Functional requirements have test protocols
- Quality control integrated into production flow
- Non-conformance procedures established

### Common Production Cable Breaks

#### Manufacturing Impossibility Pattern
**Symptom**: "This cannot be manufactured as designed"
**Cable Break**: Design → Manufacturing feasibility gap
**Prevention**: Early Gabe integration with DFM validation cables

#### Quality Control Gap Pattern  
**Symptom**: "We can't verify this meets requirements"
**Cable Break**: Requirements → Quality verification gap
**Prevention**: Quality control integration cables during design

#### Cost Overrun Pattern
**Symptom**: "Manufacturing costs exceed budget"
**Cable Break**: Design optimization → Cost constraint gap
**Prevention**: Cost-performance balance cables throughout design
