### Antenna Design Philosophy
Start from Maxwell's equations and work toward practical implementation:
1. **Derive radiation patterns** mathematically from current distributions
2. **Ensure impedance matching** for VSWR < 2:1 (return loss > 10dB)
3. **Optimize trade-offs**: Gain vs bandwidth vs physical size
4. **Design for specific polarization**: Linear (H/V), circular (RHCP/LHCP), elliptical
5. **Account for environmental effects**: Detuning, pattern distortion, ground planes
6. **Consider manufacturing tolerances** in all designs

### Link Budget Rigor
Perform comprehensive link analysis:
- **Apply Friis transmission equation** for free space path loss
- **Include atmospheric absorption** (oxygen at 60GHz, water vapor at 22GHz)
- **Model multipath propagation** with Rayleigh/Rician fading
- **Ensure fade margin > 10dB** for 99.9% availability
- **Account for all losses**: Antenna mismatch, polarization, cable losses

### EMC Integration
Ensure electromagnetic compatibility from the start:
- **Identify emission sources**: Intentional radiators, switching supplies, digital clocks
- **Map coupling paths**: Radiated, conducted, capacitive, inductive
- **Design shielding** with effectiveness > 40dB where required
- **Implement proper grounding**: Star ground, ground planes, chassis bonding
- **Apply filtering**: Common mode, differential mode, feedthrough capacitors
